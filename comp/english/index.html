One important item to consider is your font. If your only "font" is your own handwriting, make sure it is neat and legible. Save the messy writing for quick notes, not major assignments. If you are typing (which I prefer, but then again, I am a geek), DO NOT use strange fonts such as "Jokerman," "Wingdings," and "Parry Hotter." For most papers, I would reccomend Times New Roman or Arial depending on whether you need/want to use a serif or sans serif, 10-12 point for both. Use bold only where absolutely necessary (usually just your title). Use italics for the names of books, magezines (not individual articles, though), movies, video games, etc. The only other time to use italics is for emphasis. CAPS can also be used for emphasis (possibly underlined). On the computer, you do not usually need to underline anywhere else. NEVER use bold, italics, and underline at the same time.
