What is RSS?

RSS (short for Really Simple Syndication) is a popular format for “feeds” of updates. It is often used for things such as blog entries, news headlines, and podcasts. RSS feeds can be read using programs called “aggregators” (they are also sometimes called “readers”). They are convenient because they allow people to view feeds from many different sites in one place, without needing to visit all those sites separately. If you are new to the world of RSS and do not yet have a favorite aggregator, I recommend Google Reader. Just sign in with your Google Account (or create one) and then click “Browse for stuff” on the left sidebar.
